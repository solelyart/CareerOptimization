## 1. Executive Summary

Momentum Ops Consulting is a boutique consultancy founded by Kristen Blanks, designed to help small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) unlock growth through Salesforce CRM optimization and strategic process automation. We bridge the gap between technology and business objectives, transforming complex systems into streamlined, revenue-generating assets. Our core mission is to empower SMBs with the operational efficiency and data-driven insights typically reserved for large enterprises.

Based on Kristen Blanks’ proven track record of driving 35% increases in user adoption and 40% reductions in operational bottlenecks, Momentum Ops is positioned to deliver immediate, measurable ROI for its clients. We will launch as a sole proprietorship, offering a tiered menu of services from a foundational "Salesforce Health Check" to comprehensive CRM implementation and CLM integration. The initial target market will be SMBs in the technology and professional services sectors with 50-250 employees who are underutilizing their Salesforce investment.

Financial projections indicate a realistic first-year revenue of $145,000, achieved through a mix of project-based work and retainer agreements, with a net profit margin of over 70% due to the low-overhead, remote-first model.

## 3. Services Offered

## 4. Market Analysis

The demand for Salesforce expertise and digital transformation services for SMBs is experiencing explosive growth. Momentum Ops Consulting is strategically positioned to capitalize on these powerful market trends.

### Market Size & Growth

The global Salesforce consulting service market is a massive and rapidly expanding industry. Key projections validate the significant opportunity:

*   **Global Market Growth:** The market is projected to grow from **$20.93 billion in 2025 to $57.00 billion by 2032**, demonstrating a robust Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of **15.4%** [1].
*   **U.S. Market:** The United States market alone is expected to grow from **$6.5 billion in 2024 to $12.8 billion by 2033** [2].
*   **Talent Demand:** Demand for Salesforce consultants in the USA surged by **72% in 2025** after a brief dip, with consultancy roles now making up a quarter of all Salesforce job listings [5].

This data indicates a sustained, high-growth environment where specialized consultants are in high demand and can command premium rates.

### Target Market: The Underserved SMB

While large enterprises have dedicated teams and massive budgets for Salesforce, Small and Medium-sized Businesses (SMBs) represent a largely underserved segment. This is our primary target market.

*   **Digital Transformation is Key:** SMBs are increasingly recognizing that digital transformation is critical for survival and growth. Key trends for 2026 include AI, automation, and cloud computing—all areas where Momentum Ops provides value [9].
*   **The Salesforce Gap:** Many SMBs have invested in Salesforce but lack the in-house expertise to fully leverage its capabilities. They are often stuck with a basic, underutilized implementation that functions more like a simple contact database than a powerful CRM.
*   **Need for ROI:** SMBs are highly focused on ROI. They need practical, cost-effective solutions that deliver measurable results quickly. They cannot afford the multi-million dollar, multi-year projects typical of large enterprise consulting.

**Ideal Client Profile:**
*   **Industry:** Technology, Professional Services, SaaS, B2B Services
*   **Size:** 50-250 employees
*   **Revenue:** $5M - $50M annually
*   **Pain Points:**
    *   "Our Salesforce is a mess."
    *   "We aren’t getting the data we need to make decisions."
    *   "Our sales and service processes are inefficient and manual."
    *   "We know we could be doing more with Salesforce, but we don’t know how."

### Competitive Landscape

The Salesforce consulting ecosystem is diverse, ranging from global system integrators to individual freelancers. Momentum Ops will differentiate itself by occupying a strategic niche.

| Competitor Type | Examples | Strengths | Weaknesses | How Momentum Ops Competes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Global SIs** | Accenture, Deloitte, PwC | Deep resources, large teams, established enterprise relationships | High cost, slow-moving, bureaucratic, not focused on SMBs | By being agile, affordable, and exclusively focused on the needs of SMBs. We offer a personalized, hands-on approach they cannot match. |
| **Boutique Consultancies** | FoundHQ freelancers, regional partners | Specialized expertise, more affordable than SIs | Variable quality, may lack strategic business acumen | By combining deep technical expertise with a strong focus on business strategy and measurable ROI. Our founder’s background as a Business Systems Analyst is a key differentiator. |
| **Individual Freelancers** | Upwork, Toptal | Low cost, flexible | Often tactical (e.g., "build this one flow") rather than strategic, may lack a holistic business perspective, reliability can be a concern | By offering a professional, structured, and reliable service with a clear methodology and guaranteed outcomes. We are a strategic partner, not just a temporary coder. |

**Our Unique Selling Proposition (USP):**
> Momentum Ops Consulting is the strategic partner for SMBs who want to unlock the full potential of their Salesforce investment. We combine the strategic insight of a large consultancy with the agility and cost-effectiveness of a freelancer, delivering practical, high-ROI solutions that drive measurable growth.

## 5. Strategy & Implementation

Our go-to-market strategy is focused on building authority, establishing trust, and creating a scalable client acquisition engine.

### Marketing & Sales Strategy

*   **Phase 1: Foundational (Months 1-3)**
    *   **Professional Website:** Launch a clean, professional website showcasing our services, case studies (initially based on past career achievements), and client testimonials.
    *   **LinkedIn Optimization:** Create a powerful LinkedIn presence for both Kristen Blanks and Momentum Ops Consulting. Regularly post valuable content (tips, case studies, industry insights) to build a following.
    *   **Niche Freelance Marketplaces:** Establish a strong presence on **FoundHQ** and **Braintrust** to capture immediate project opportunities.

*   **Phase 2: Content & Authority (Months 4-9)**
    *   **Content Marketing:** Publish one in-depth blog post or case study per month. Topics will focus on solving common SMB pain points (e.g., "5 Signs Your Salesforce Needs a Health Check," "How to Automate Your Contract Renewal Process").
    *   **Webinars:** Host one free webinar per quarter on a high-value topic, such as "Salesforce for SMBs: From Cost Center to Revenue Driver." Use this to build an email list.
    *   **Networking:** Actively participate in online Salesforce communities and local business groups.

*   **Phase 3: Scaling (Months 10-18)**
    *   **Referral Program:** Implement a formal referral program for existing clients.
    *   **Paid Advertising:** Experiment with targeted LinkedIn ads to promote webinars and Tier 1 Health Check services.
    *   **Partnerships:** Develop relationships with complementary businesses (e.g., marketing agencies, accounting firms) for cross-referrals.

### Operations Plan

*   **Technology Stack:**
    *   **CRM:** A free Salesforce Developer Edition to manage our own client pipeline.
    *   **Project Management:** Jira or Trello for managing client projects.
    *   **Communication:** Slack and Zoom for client collaboration.
    *   **Website:** Webflow or Squarespace for a professional, low-maintenance site.
    *   **Accounting:** QuickBooks Self-Employed.

*   **Client Onboarding Process:**
    1.  **Initial Consultation (Free):** 30-minute discovery call to understand pain points.
    2.  **Proposal:** Deliver a detailed proposal outlining scope, timeline, and pricing.
    3.  **Contract:** Use a standardized consulting agreement.
    4.  **Kick-off Meeting:** Formal project kick-off to align on goals and expectations.

## 8. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

Every business faces risks. Momentum Ops has identified the following key risks and developed mitigation strategies.

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Difficulty acquiring first clients** | Medium | High | • Offer discounted "beta" projects to build portfolio<br>• Leverage warm network from past roles<br>• Use freelance marketplaces (FoundHQ, Braintrust) for immediate opportunities |
| **Cash flow gaps between projects** | Medium | Medium | • Prioritize retainer clients for predictable revenue<br>• Maintain 3-month emergency fund<br>• Offer flexible payment terms (50% upfront, 50% on completion) |
| **Scope creep on fixed-price projects** | High | Medium | • Use detailed Statements of Work<br>• Implement change order process<br>• Build 10-15% buffer into project timelines |
| **Burnout from solo operations** | Medium | High | • Set clear boundaries (no work on weekends)<br>• Outsource non-core tasks (bookkeeping, admin)<br>• Plan for subcontractor support by Month 12 |
| **Market saturation/competition** | Low | Medium | • Focus on underserved SMB niche<br>• Differentiate through business strategy focus<br>• Build strong personal brand and thought leadership |
| **Economic downturn affecting SMB budgets** | Low | High | • Diversify client base across industries<br>• Emphasize ROI and cost savings in messaging<br>• Offer smaller, modular services (Tier 1-2) during downturns |
