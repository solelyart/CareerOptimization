# Virtual Assistant Trial: Evaluation & Hiring Questions

**Purpose:** Use these questions to screen and evaluate VA services before starting the 10-hour trial engagement.

**When to Use:** During initial conversations with VA services or freelancers on platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, BELAY, or Fancy Hands.

### Section 1: Experience & Qualifications

**1. Platform Setup Experience**
> "Have you set up professional profiles on job boards, freelance platforms, or tutoring websites before? Which platforms?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Specific platform names (Indeed, LinkedIn, Upwork, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Number of clients helped with similar tasks

**2. Attention to Detail**
> "How do you ensure 100% accuracy when transferring information from a resume to multiple platform profiles?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Mentions QA process or checklists
- ‚úÖ Uses tools (e.g., spell check, Grammarly)
- ‚úÖ Double-checks dates, titles, company names
- ‚úÖ Cross-references original documents

- ‚ùå "I'm just careful" without process
- ‚ùå No mention of verification steps
- ‚ùå Defensive about mistakes

**3. Communication Style**
> "If you encounter a missing piece of information (like a specific date or certification), how would you handle it?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Asks clarifying questions immediately
- ‚úÖ Doesn't guess or assume
- ‚úÖ Documents questions in organized way
- ‚úÖ Proposes solutions when asking

**Example Good Answer:**
> "I would pause that section, document it in my questions list, and send you a message like: 'I need the exact date you earned your ECBA certification‚Äîthe resume shows 2019, but Braintrust requires month/year. Can you confirm?'"

**4. Tech Savviness**
> "What tools do you use for password management, project tracking, and file organization?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Uses LastPass, 1Password, or similar for credentials
- ‚úÖ Knows Trello, Asana, or Google Sheets for tracking
- ‚úÖ Organizes files systematically (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- ‚úÖ Comfortable with screenshots and documentation

**Red Flags:**
- ‚ùå Stores passwords in plain text files
- ‚ùå No project tracking system
- ‚ùå Disorganized file management

---

**5. Time Management**
> "For this trial, I estimate 5-6 hours for 3 platforms. Does that align with your experience? How do you track time?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Estimates are realistic (not too fast or too slow)
- ‚úÖ Uses time tracking tools (Toggl, Clockify, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Breaks down time per platform
- ‚úÖ Builds in buffer for QA

**Example Good Answer:**
> "Yes, that's realistic. I'd estimate 1.5 hrs for Indeed, 2 hrs for Wyzant (more fields), and 2.5 hrs for LinkedIn optimization. I use Toggl to track time per platform."

### Section 2: Work Style & Logistics

**6. Availability & Timeline**
> "This trial needs to be completed within 7 days. Can you commit to this timeline? What are your working hours?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Clear availability (e.g., "M-F, 9am-5pm EST")
- ‚úÖ Can meet 7-day deadline comfortably
- ‚úÖ Has backup plan if delayed
- ‚úÖ Transparent about other commitments

**Red Flags:**
- ‚ùå Vague availability
- ‚ùå Already overbooked
- ‚ùå Different time zone with no overlap

---

**7. Response Time**
> "During the trial, I'll need answers to questions within 4-6 hours (business days). Is that manageable for you?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Commits to specific response time
- ‚úÖ Has system for notifications (phone, email alerts)
- ‚úÖ Proactively mentions if offline (weekends, holidays)

---

**8. Quality vs. Speed**
> "If you had to choose between finishing faster with minor errors OR taking extra time to ensure perfection, which would you choose?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Chooses quality over speed
- ‚úÖ Mentions QA process
- ‚úÖ Would rather ask questions than guess

**Good Answer:**
> "I'd take extra time to ensure accuracy. It's faster to do it right the first time than to redo it. I always do a final QA check before submitting."

---

### Section 3: Trial-Specific Questions

**9. Understanding of Trial Purpose**
> "Why do you think I'm starting with a 10-hour trial instead of hiring for the full project right away?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Understands it's a test of fit/quality
- ‚úÖ Not offended by trial structure
- ‚úÖ Sees value in proving capability
- ‚úÖ Mentions building trust

**Red Flag Answer:**
- ‚ùå "I don't usually do trials"
- ‚ùå Seems insulted or defensive

---

**10. Success Criteria**
> "What does success look like to you for this trial?"

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Mentions quality (100% complete, no errors)
- ‚úÖ Mentions communication (responsive, clear)
- ‚úÖ Mentions client satisfaction
- ‚úÖ Wants to convert trial to full project

**Example Good Answer:**
> "Success means: 1) All 3 platforms are 100% complete with zero errors, 2) You're impressed with quality and communication, 3) You feel confident hiring me for the remaining 12 platforms."

---

- ‚úÖ Understands professional tone
- ‚úÖ Completes within time limit

---

**12. Detail Orientation Test**
> "Here's a sample profile section with 5 intentional errors. Can you spot them?" (Provide sample with typos, date errors, formatting issues)

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ Finds all 5 errors
- ‚úÖ Explains what's wrong and how to fix
- ‚úÖ Completes quickly (under 10 minutes)

## üí¨ Sample Conversation Flow

> "Hi [VA Name],
>
> I'm looking for a VA to help set up profiles on 15 professional platforms (job boards, freelance sites, tutoring platforms). Before committing to the full project, I'd like to start with a 10-hour trial covering 3 platforms.

> 1. I'll send you the Trial Proposal and SOW to review
